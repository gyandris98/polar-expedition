Frequently Asked Questions
About Our Game

How many players can play?

A minimum of 3, maximum of 6.

How do you play the game?

Each of the selected characters can perform 4 actions, which can be selected on the bottom left list after each other. The actions can be stepping from an icefield to another, digging the snow layers on the field they're standing on, digging out the item frozen in the ice, picking it up, building a tent (if you have the tent item in your inventory), assembling the flare gun (if all players stand on a single icefield and have all 3 parts between them), and using the special skill (building an igloo or discovering how many players can a neighbouring icefield hold). If a character's bodyheat reaches 0, somebody falls in water or finds themselves on the same field, as the icebear, the game is lost. You win by assembling and firing the flare gun to be rescued!

How are the eskimo and explorer different?

The eskimo is a seasoned inhabitant of the arctic and is more resistant to cold; they start with 5 bodyheat. They can also build an igloo, which protects all players on the same icefield from the cold of the snowstorms and against an icebear attack.
The explorer only starts with 4 bodyheat, but has the ability to discover how many players an icefield is able to hold, in order to ensure safe passage for the team.

How do I step / explore?

Choose the step / explore action on the bottom left, then click on one of your character's neighbouring fields. You will find the capacity of the explored icefield as a message at the top of the screen.

Why did my bodyheat just go down? Why did a previously dug icefield become snowy again?

Before each player's turn, there's a chance of a snowstorm occurring. This will reduce each character's bodyheat by one, if they're not on a field that has an igloo or tent on it and cover some icefields with more snow.

What do the items do?

A tent will protect each player on the icefield from the cold of a snowstorm for a round (igloo does the same, but is permanent), if you have a rope, and someone falls in the water on a neighbouring field, you'll automatically rescue them. Food will give you an extra bodyheat when you pick it up, the diving suit will protect you from drowning in the water, and you can dig twice as much snow with each action if you have a shovel. A weaker, wooden shovel will break after three uses. The pistol, cartridge and flare are parts that have to be assembled to win the game!
